Declan's Presentation

It is the end of the quarter. THE RISE OF SKYWALKER is approaching. JJ ABRAMS has reached out to ask: "How do you make a great STAR WARS film?"

Armed with a new DATA VIZ understanding, it is Declan's job to analyze the recent previous films and find out what should be done in order to maximize success.

STAR WARS

Suggestions for JJ

In order to maximize your profits, I would make sure of a couple things. Firstly, the movie must be released during Christmas time. Three of the four Star Wars movies of this generation have been released during Christmas, and those three are the best performers. Star Wars works well as a Christmas event, as families are together and can go see it, increasing revenue. You can also sell much more merchandise due to the nature of the holidays.

The next thing to do is to diversify your cast. Rogue One had a great cast, full of wonderful actors, and only the lead female was white. The rest of the actors are Chinese, Mexican, Pakistani, and a whole other host of cultures. Representation matters, and it is clear when comparing Rogue One's box office to The Last Jedi, and episodical film that only outperformed it by a little.

It is also important to focus advertisement heavily in the USA and UK. These are the top two performing countries for box office, so they should be prioritized. Australia and China are also quite large as well, so providing a lot of focus there would be great.

A Star Wars film should also be dense. The Force Awakens and Rogue One are the two top performers for the new films. Both of these barely give you a break to think. They are paced very quickly, moving from one place to the next. This keeps you engaged and actively interested. Both of these have the highest percentage of planets shown out of the new films. These two are also about two hours and fifteen minutes long, whereas The Last Jedi clocks in at around two and a half hours. These extra fifteen minutes, if not used well, can put people off returning for another viewing.
